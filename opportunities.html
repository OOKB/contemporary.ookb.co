2015-16 Internship Program

The Contemporary seeks three interns to contribute to our team for one whole academic year, starting in September 2015 and ending May 2016. The internship provides an opportunity for individuals to learn about the operation of a nomadic, non-collecting museum focused on site-specific and subject-oriented projects in Baltimore. Interns will work closely with our small staff to carry out projects and facilitate our additional programming such as our Speaker Series that brings internationally- recognized artists, critics, curators, and art professionals to Baltimore. Interns are responsible for producing an issue of our annual intern publication, Scroll, under the guidance of museum staff.

The Contemporary offers each intern a stipend of $1000. Additionally, academic credit may be arranged with the internâ€™s college/university. Student status, however, is not a prerequisite for consideration. Internships begin in September and end in May (a duration of 9 months or one academic year) with an expected time commitment averaging 10 hours per week. Interns will collaborate to conceptualize, create, design, and produce one issue of our annual research-based publication, Scroll. Each issue explores a cultural topic relevant to the city of Baltimore and the mission of The Contemporary. Scroll will be available for free to the public in print and online.

Interns will be treated as staff members and will assist the museum with routine work as well as special projects. Other intern responsibilities include but are not limited to administrative tasks, archiving, digitization, assisting during museum events, and attending field trips.

The ideal intern is highly motivated, curious, and has a strong interest in Baltimore, contemporary art, and our mission to expand the role of the museum as incubator and engage diverse audiences. The candidate should have excellent computer, organizational, design, and interpersonal skills, as well as effective reading and writing skills.
